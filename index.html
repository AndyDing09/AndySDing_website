About Me

Photo Coming Soon

Hi! I'm Andy, a 10th grader at Weston High School (Class of 2028) with a passion for engineering, environmental science, and building things that matter. I thrive at the intersection of hardware, software, and real-world impact — whether that's designing autonomous vehicles, developing medical imaging systems, or competing on a national stage.

Beyond research, I captain my FTC robotics team, compete in DECA and debate, swim at the varsity level, and write about my work for public audiences.

Research

Exploring problems at the frontier of computer science.

Ongoing 2025–Present

Project Kymarion — ISEF Autonomous Surface Vehicle

A collaborative ISEF research project investigating whether nearshore microplastic concentrations peak at sites where opposing coastal currents converge. We designed and built a low-cost autonomous surface vehicle equipped with GPS-tagged sensors that conducts 15–20 waypoint surveys per deployment across candidate convergence zones along the Massachusetts coast. Statistical analysis (ANOVA, regression against convergence-strength metrics) will test whether microplastic accumulation is governed by predictable hydrodynamic features — and if confirmed, identify high-yield sites for future capture stations.

ISEF — Environmental Science & Earth/Ocean Sciences · Co-investigator

Ongoing Summer 2026

LuminaBone — Low-Cost Shading-Based 3D Bone Endoscope

A medical imaging project developing a 6 mm endoscope that uses near-light photometric stereo to reconstruct accurate 3D bone topography in real time — without dye or expensive stereo cameras. Two off-axis micro-LEDs driven sequentially via PWM capture shading gradients across rigid bone surfaces; a Lambertian + inverse-square pipeline then solves surface normals and integrates them into a full 3D map. Targeting ≥15 fps on bone phantoms and validated against high-precision laser scans.
