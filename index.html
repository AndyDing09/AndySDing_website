Research

Independent research in environmental engineering, robotics, and ocean science.

Ongoing 2025 – Present

Project Kymarion — ISEF Autonomous Surface Vehicle

A collaborative ISEF research project investigating whether nearshore microplastic concentrations peak at sites where opposing coastal currents converge. We designed and built a low-cost autonomous surface vehicle equipped with GPS-tagged sensors that conducts 15–20 waypoint surveys per deployment across candidate convergence zones along the Massachusetts coast. Statistical analysis will test whether microplastic accumulation is governed by predictable hydrodynamic features.

ISEF — Environmental Science & Earth/Ocean Sciences · Co-investigator Learn more →
Ongoing Summer 2026

LuminaBone — Low-Cost Shading-Based 3D Bone Endoscope

A medical imaging project developing a 6 mm endoscope that uses near-light photometric stereo to reconstruct accurate 3D bone topography in real time — without dye or expensive stereo cameras. Two off-axis micro-LEDs driven sequentially via PWM capture shading gradients across rigid bone surfaces; a Lambertian + inverse-square pipeline then solves surface normals and integrates them into a full 3D map. Targeting ≥15 fps on bone phantoms and validated against high-precision laser scans.

Summer 2026 Medical Imaging Internship — orthopedic & ENT applications
